Nutrition

What you eat affects how you feel!
Believe it or not!
So, here are a few tips of what to put in your body that nutritional therapists have advised that can make you FEEL good!
There’s also a list of food that you may want to consume less which may make you feel not as good.

Feel good foods

1. Berries

Blueberries, raspberries, and strawberries are all high in vitamin C, which helps cope with stress. It helps clear out cortisol a hormone that is released during stressful periods.
You know what fruit to snack on when it comes to revising for exams now!

Black beans, lentils, and lima beans are all rich in magnesium, a mineral that functions to provide relaxation and calm.

3. Dark Chocolate

Everyone loves a bit of chocolate. And if you don’t then you’re just a bit weird. The very taste of chocolate can produce a near euphoric state in many people. Dark chocolate is full of protein and fibre and supports a positive mood and healthy cognition.
Does that mean dark chocolate makes people smarter?

4. Fish

Mackerel, salmon, sardines, and trout all have high amounts of omega-3 fatty acids, which can alleviate anxiety.
Next time you feel a state of panic, maybe try fishing!

5. Herbal Teas

Many herbal teas, such as chamomile, have calming properties. Black, green, white and red (rooibos) teas are also rich in antioxidants. Drinking a cup of warm tea can relieve stress and lift the spirits.
Everyone feels better after a cuppa! A herbal cuppa!

6. Leafy Greens

Kale is loaded with mood-moderating magnesium and raw spinach contains vital nutrients. They prevent cognitive decline.
Pop-eye the sailor was always right then!

7. Whole Fruits

Apples, bananas, and oranges are packed with fiber and vitamin C. The sweet tastes and aromatic smells of these fruits can brighten one’s outlook and promote well-being.
Did you know that instead of 5-a-day, nutritional therapists recommend having 10-a-day!

Feel Not So Good Foods

1. Caffeine

Caffeine can trigger the body's fight-or-flight response. Very similar to the symptoms associated with anxiety, caffeine can also make its users feel nervous, nauseous, light headed, jittery and yes, even anxious.
If you have anxiety, I recommend reconsidering that morning cup of coffee and instead opt for an herbal tea or a smoothie with some leafy greens!

1. Alcohol

Alcohol in excess or at all (for some), is said to induce the symptoms of anxiety. Alcohol is a toxin that leads to improper mental and physical functioning, by negatively impacting the levels of serotonin (the feel good chemical) in the brain
Think before you drink!

2. Gluten

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley and rye products. Gluten can also be a huge trigger for anxiety symptoms. Cutting out gluten could be the difference between living with anxiety and managing it for the better
Gluten can be associated with carbs and generally brown carbs have been seen to be better than white carbs.

3. Sugars

Sugars- artificial and refined can create changes in your body that can exacerbate anxiety symptoms and impairs the body's ability to effectively cope with stress.
A sugar crash is similar to a caffeine crash, and can also cause mood changes, heart palpitations, difficulty concentrating and fatigue— all of which can be interpreted as the beginning stages of an anxiety or panic attack.
