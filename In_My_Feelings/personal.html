Personal

Coping strategies include focusing and loving yourself by getting to know yourself and what you enjoy.
See below for some of our suggestions!

GRATTITUDE JOURNAL

Writing your thoughts and feelings down is another form of therapy. Focusing on what you are grateful for by either words or pictures is another way to be more positive!

HOBBIES

Doing what you like, in your free time, is a good way of occupying your mind and at the same time - having fun! You can do this alone or with friends. Hobbies can be baking or cooking, creative writing, painting or even taking part in a sport!

ANIMALS

Pets have proven to be a great source of comfort, companionship, and motivation to people. Being around animals has proven to have calming effects for people that suffer from anxiety and depression. We understand that sometimes owning a pet can be a bit difficult and so some options for people include a voluntary walking dog service such as ”Borrow my Doggy” app or volunteering in your local dog or cat shelter.
